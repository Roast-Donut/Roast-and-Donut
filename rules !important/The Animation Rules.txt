To bring the Roast and Donut aesthetic to life, the animations must feel "organic yet mechanical"—like a high-end watch or a perfectly optimized iOS app. Because we are avoiding bold fonts, we use movement and spacing to create hierarchy.

Here is the comprehensive guide on how every element should behave, mapped to your database architecture.

1. The "Weightless" Page Entry
When a user navigates to the Main Feed or Trending Feed, the page should not just "appear."

Staggered Fade-In: Bento grid cards from v_main_feed should slide up 10px and fade in one by one (staggered by 0.05s).

Dot Grid Background: The #E5E5E5 dots should gently fade in last, creating the feeling that the content has landed on a technical workspace.

2. Interactive Text & Hover States
Since we have zero bold fonts, the transition from #737373 to #000 is your primary "Signal" to the user.

The "Ink" Transition: When hovering over a title or author_name, the color change to #000 should take exactly 0.2s with a "linear" curve to feel crisp.

Micro-Scaling: On hover, the text should also scale by a tiny factor (1.02x) to create depth without adding weight.

Link Underlines: Never use standard underlines. Use a 1px #000 bottom border that "grows" from the center outward when hovered.

3. Bento Grid "Magic" Reorganization
The grid should feel alive. When a user interacts with a card, the layout adapts.

The Expansion Flow: When a user clicks a post from the v_trending_feed to see comments, the card should expand using a Layout Projection (like Framer Motion).

Push & Pull: Neighboring cards should gently slide away to make room for the expanded content, rather than disappearing.

The "Lift" Effect: On hover, a card (#FFF) should lift up 4px and its subtle 1px border should change from #E5E5E5 to #737373.

4. Image & Asset Behaviors
Images from your user_assets or cover_image_url should feel premium.

Soft Zoom: When hovering over a post card, the cover_image_url should slowly zoom in (1.05x) inside its container.

Glassmorphism Reveal: Overlays (like the "Burn Timer" or "Heat Index") should use a backdrop-filter: blur(12px) and only appear when the user hovers over the image.

Grayscale to Color: Images should sit at 50% saturation. On hover, they should smoothly transition to full color to emphasize active engagement.

5. Data-Driven "System" Animations
Because you have high-level logic in your DB, use animations to visualize it.

The Burn Countdown: If a post has a burn_at timestamp, show a tiny 1px horizontal progress bar at the bottom of the card that slowly "erodes" as the time approaches.

Heat Map Pulsing: In the v_roast_heat_map, countries with high roast_count should have a soft, #737373 circular pulse radiating from their center.

Interaction "Pop": When clicking a "Star" or "Donut" in post_interactions, the icon should briefly scale up to 1.4x and back down to 1.0x in 0.1s to confirm the action.

6. Loading & Error States
Skeleton Grids: Instead of a spinner, use empty #F5F5F5 bento boxes that have a shimmering horizontal light effect.

Smooth Error Shakes: If a login fails (failed_attempts), the bento box should do a subtle 5px horizontal shake—no red colors, just a quick movement to signal "Try again."
